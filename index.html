Research Engineer · MBZUAI

Kartik Kuckreja

I work on trustworthy multimodal AI, with a focus on synthetic media detection and interpretability. My current research is on deepfake detectors whose predictions are supported by verifiable visual evidence, and on evaluating whether the explanations such models produce are faithful to the manipulations actually present.

I am a Research Engineer at MBZUAI, advised by Prof. Muhammad Haris Khan and Prof. Abhinav Dhall (Monash University).

Synthetic media detection

Datasets and models for detecting manipulated audio-visual media, including multilingual and code-switched speech (ArEnAV), regionally diverse faces (INDIFACE), and million-scale benchmarks with real-world perturbations (AV-Deepfake1M++).

related papers

Interpretability

Methods for testing whether a detector's explanation reflects the manipulation that is actually present: reasoning supervision with MLLM judges (DeepfakeJudge) and large-scale evaluation of explanation faithfulness (XPlainVerse).

related papers

Evaluation of multimodal models

Benchmarks that measure model behavior where failures carry real cost: cultural and linguistic coverage across 100 languages (ALM-Bench) and geospatial reasoning (GEOBench-VLM).
