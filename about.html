About

I am a Research Engineer at the Mohamed bin Zayed University of Artificial Intelligence (MBZUAI) in Abu Dhabi, advised by Prof. Muhammad Haris Khan and Prof. Abhinav Dhall (Monash University). My research is on trustworthy multimodal AI — in particular, interpretable models for synthetic media detection, and methods for testing whether the explanations such models produce are faithful to the evidence.

I studied Computer Science at BITS Pilani, Hyderabad Campus. For my undergraduate thesis I built GeoChat, the first grounded large vision-language model for remote sensing, supervised by Prof. Fahad Shahbaz Khan, Dr. Salman Khan and Dr. Abhijit Das; the work was published at CVPR 2024. Before that, I spent a summer at IIT Ropar's LASII Lab with Dr. Abhinav Dhall, where I first started working on deepfake detection.

Since then my work has covered deepfake datasets and detection, explanation faithfulness, multilingual and cultural evaluation of multimodal models, and vision-language models for earth observation.
